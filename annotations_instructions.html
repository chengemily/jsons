Overall goals

  1. Clean up and standardize player data (it is quite noisy as it is self-submitted)
  2. Track perception and understanding of signs by players
  3. Classify signs into index, icon, and symbol.

Navigation

  1. One spreadsheet tab = one pair of subjects with pair ID .
  2. Player-submitted data (left = teacher, right = student): global observations about a task, sign drawing + meaning used in the task ("signaries").
  3. If tasks end before 44 (total # of tasks), it's because the pair withdrew at that time.

Instructions

Data cleaning

  1. Problem: players register multiple signs for the same sign. E.g. "TOUCH RED" and "TOUCH BLUE" should be absorbed into "TOUCH". To do: For each sign, enter a unique ID in the ID column, starting with 0 and incrementing with each new sign. When signs are changed, (they'll be marked "CHANGED"), assign the sign a new number.
  2. Problem: players don't register new or previously used signs but describe them in observations. To do: add a sign + its meaning (taking verbatim if possible). E.g. Signs: "TOUCH" + Meanings: "Go to each of the objects. I think the student understood quickly"

Perception and Understanding

  1. To do: Annotate each sign in "Score" column with 1, 2, or 3 using context clues from observations and signaries.
    1. 1: Un-noticed by other player
    2. 2: Noticed but not understood
    3. 3: Noticed and understood
  2. Note: Sometimes players will un-understand or un-notice a sign. Just put the number independently of whether it was previously understood or noticed.

Indices, icons, and symbols

  1. Goal: classify all signs into indices, icons, and symbols. This can be done once per sign when it's introduced.
  2. To do: Annotate each sign in "Cat" column with 1, 2, and/or 3 according to the rubric. If a sign meets multiple criteria, put multiple numbers ordered by salience. E.g. if it's mostly symbolic and also iconic, put "32".
    1. 1: Does the sign "point" to its meaning? ("index"). E.g. TOUCH
    2. 2: Does the sign physically look like its meaning? E.g. TRIANGLE, BACKWARDS ("icon")
    3. 3: Does the sign's shape have no obvious relationship to its meaning? (More likely when the meaning is abstract or when the sign is abstract). E.g. NO ("symbol")
